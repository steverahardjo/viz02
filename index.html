Foreign Arrivals to Malaysia (Jan 2020 - Jun 2024)

According to records from Malaysia's Foreign Affairs, foreign nationals have entered Malaysia using various modes of transportation from January 2020 to June 2024, accumulating to 2 million records, with Singapore citizens dominating the entries, as you can see from the flow map and the heat map (Johor). By looking at the overall timeline, we can see there are ticks in the overall flow of arrivals caused by the worldwide immigration restrictions due to COVID-19, and a gradual uptick from Eastern Europe caused by the Russo-Ukrainian War. These numbers can be correlated to the 0.3 % of GDP.
These foreign arrivals are not just created by tourism demand alone but also by the continuous trend of Malaysians relying on foreign workers, mainly in the 3D sector (Dirty, Dangerous, and Demeaning) with 3.3 million right now officially documented in Malaysia, with many more entries illegally, which is around 15% compared to the Malaysian population where most of them are from Indonesia, Bangladesh, and Myanmar This shows how dependent the Malaysian economy is on foreign influx.

Citizens from various countries visit Malaysia for several reasons, reflecting key factors that influence their travel decisions. Geographical proximity plays a significant role, particularly with , Singapore,where strong collaboration leads to daily intercommutes, especially in hotspots like Johor bahru. Similar patterns can be seen with Thailand. Cultural connections are also crucial, with Malaysia's rich heritage attracting tourists from China and India. Additionally, Malaysia is an appealing destination due to its unique climate and vibrant culture, coupled with lower living costs. The Big Mac Index and GDP per Capita highlights this advantage, showing that Malaysia offers more affordable living compared to countries Japan, South Korea, and Australia.

USA are an outlier in term of tourist arrival , thi is because usually a large distance will equate with higher traveling cost which hinder tourist to come, but this is not the case for United States visitors.
